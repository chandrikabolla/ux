Indeed website Case study is my personal project after graduation. I used user research methods such as user inyerviews, user surveys, card sorting, competitor analysis, usability testing during this project.

Project

Design Challenges: Consistency, Changes in design that can increase conversion rate, Smooth process flow for profile, resume edits, adding new features that differentiate the website from its competitors

Role: Designer and Researcher

Timeline: 10 days

Process

Discovery

User Research Interviews: I began my process by conducting ethnographic field study followed by user research interviews (semi-structured) with my classmates and recently graduated friends (aged 20-27), who were looking for jobs online. I concluded the research after discovering pain points with the user flow and application process on the website. An overview of their responses is like this:

“indeed.com is like Google for the job search. It is simple by looks and good for basic search results but it can be better.”


Survey: Although user research interviews gave me a detailed and quality research output, I conducted a user research survey to analyze and validate my research quantitatively. Using OptimalSurvey, I conducted survey with 30 users by asking questions about their experience with the website, questions related to features on the webiste.

I validated my qualitative data with quantitative data without relying on just either one of them. The survey data helped me in reorganizing the filters for jobsearch on the website. On the whole, the research from discovery phase gave me ideas, inspiration for new features for the site.


Interpretation

Competitor Analysis: I started with identifying the competitors in the product landscape. Then, I conducted competitor research and analysis with the website's major competitors of the website, Linkedin.com, monster.com, glassdoor.com, careerbuilder.com. My research and analysis was focused on ux workflow for applying job on board aka easy apply jobs, user onboarding process, product features. I used intelligence tools such as SimilarWeb.com and Google Analytics to analyze user demographics, social media traffic, languages of the compeitor websites.

SWOT Analysis: Regardless of its competitors, I analysed the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats of the website of its own.


Ideation

From research, analysis and brainstorming, I decided to add the below features in the new design. Baseline features:

  • Job application filters according to user needs
  • Uploading files from device and drive in Messages, resume
  • My jobs, my resume everything of users into a consistent My account page
  • Separating contexts for different types of users
Performance features:
  • Uploading different resumes on indeed
  • Steps in easy apply job applications
  • Creating alerts for recent job searches
  • Managing recent job searches
  • Advanced search in the home page
Delighters:
  • Careers feature
  • Job Fairs feature

Information Architecture: By following IA design model from the book "Information Architecture" by Louis Rosenfeld, my research for building IA strategy included Background research, Heuristic Evaluation, Benchmarking, use cases, Personas, User Interviews, card sorting, User Testing. I improved labeling, navigation system in the information architecture of the website.

Closed Card Sorting: Unlike the initial card sorting(open), once the information architecture strategy is designed, I conducted closed card sorting to test the design of information architecture with 10 of my users on OptimalSort.


User Personas From the research phase, I performed user segmentation based on the user's needs. I created personas for each of the user segment of the product.


Prototyping

Paper prototyping: I started prototyping on paper from the initial stage of ideation phase while brainstorming. A final paper prototype that can be referred during the later stages was designed after designing the information architecture.


Wireframes: During the design stage of information architecture, I designed low-fidelity wireframes to show the information architecture of the website. I later used these templates to design high-fidelity prototypes for user testing. I made changes in the design accummulating new basic, performance features and delighters.


Interactive Hi-fidelity Prototypes: I designed high fidelity prototypes using proto.io. I reused the low-fidelity wireframes from the previous phase for prototyping. Keeping the color palette of the original website same, I optimized the user flows and added new features.


Testing

Usability Testing By recruiting 6 participants 3 from each user segment of the product, I conducted in-person usability testing using scenarios and tasks.

Feedback The feedback was positive.

  • The users felt the new job application(steps) process is useful
  • The filters of the job search are easy to use at hand
  • The users felt confident about job search process on the website

Technology

Proto.io,OptimalWorkshop for survey and card-sorting

Challenges & Lessons Learned

  • Splitting the context of the webiste for different kinds of audiences (Jobseekers,employers, jobfair hosts - a newly added feature) was initially challenging. But this strategy puts all the users at ease while working on indeed.
  • It was difficult to gather what all the website was offering because the site has inconsistent navigation system. Then, I understood how users feel lost while browsing on the site.
  • I spent my intial days of the project on user research to gather more user insights than required. I should have spent less and concentrated more on other research methods also.

The FST website is a question and answer and blog website that allows FST students to ask questions and answer other's questions, write articles/posts on the blog after receiving certain number of votes on the website.

Project

Context: The project was designed to present an entrepreneurial idea at the Food Technology Entrepreneurial Summit 2018 held in Vizag, India.

Problem: Food Science and Technology(I will call it FST, in this report), being very less known field in education in India and due to lack of resources on web in its domain, its students face many hurdles - to follow their passion, to do their assignments in everyday academic life, to increase knowledge in the domain.

Goal: The goal of this project is to deliver a medium-to-high fidelity wireframe that shows a clear way of solving the existing problem faced by FST students.

Role: UX Designer, Researcher, Business Analyst

Timeline: 2 weeks

Process

Discovery:

User Research Interviews: I started off with user research interviews with 10 students, who are potential users of the app. During these semi-structured interviews, I listened to their stories, learned about their current approach of solving the problem, the tools they were using.
Sample of responses:
“Had I known this a little earlier when I got into this program, I would have switched to another one.”
“I often can’t find information that I want. I write down the little that I find. And then, I feel no confidence in me during my presentations.”
“We often waste time reading articles that contain more content from other domains and very less of ours.”
“I feel no confidence in getting a job because I don't know the current trends in the industry.”

Focus groups: Using a screener, I filtered 10 participants out of 30 for the first focus group to discuss about the problem they are facing, how they are trying to solve the current problem and how they feel about the whole process. The second focus group consisted of 5 members from the previous 10 who were able to articulate the pain points of current method and were knowledgeable about the issue. The focus group revealed the pain points of their information search, gave me insight about their attitudes, behavior.

Researching the domain: I researched the domain and asked the Food Science students about the field. This helped me understand in the first place that there are not so many resources to get information on this domain, the field’s work varies from country to country due to difference in food cultures. This helped me to make decisions to make it local to only one country, India. And, the languages the website must be built. Like these, the initial research helped me make other decisions about content of the product.


Interpreting

Competitor Analysis: I conducted competitor research and analysis with three of the similar products stackexchange.com, quora.com, reddit.com. My research and analysis included product landscape, user demographics, product features, social media presence, social media traffic, language and content using SEO tools such as SimilarWeb.com.

User Personas:From the stories I have heard from users during the user interviews, I created personas based on their goals. I created one primary persona who would be an ambassedor of our solution and two other secondary personas who would need and use the app regularly.


Ideating

Card sorting: I performed card sorting for two times. During the design of Information Architecture, I used open card sorting to know the user thought patterns while looking for information and closed card sorting after designing the IA to validate the IA design. Both the times, it was remote card sorting as the users were remote. I used optimalcardsort of oprtimalworkshop.com to make it work. The standardization matrix showed that 60-70% of the users’ sorting matched the IA designed. Making a little improvement in the IA after card sorting analysis, I moved on creating paper prototypes.


Information Architecture:

Paper Prototypes:

Wireframes: I designed interactive wireframes after designing Information Architecture. I tested the design remotely with the users and iterated the design process by buidling high-fidelity prototypes after making a few changes.


Prototyping

Testing

Remote Moderated Usability Testing: I conducted remote usability testing as all of my users were remote. Some of the tasks in my usability testing include:

  • Log in or create account if you don't have one
  • Comment on a question that you are interested to learn more details on
  • Answer a question from Confectionary
